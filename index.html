About This Course

This portfolio documents my journey in Quantitative Methods. It includes problem-solving with optimization, statistics, forecasting, and a midterm case study. Each part reflects both technical applications and my personal learning process.

Unit 1 - Optimization (Excel Solver)

In Unit 1, I applied Linear Programming (LP) using Excel Solver. Example: A bakery produces cakes and cookies with limited ingredients (flour & sugar). Objective: Maximize profit subject to resource constraints. The Solver tool provided the optimal production mix.

Interpretation: Solver identified the most profitable product allocation while considering constraints. This shows how businesses can make better decisions with limited resources.

Unit 2 - Statistics & Forecasting

In Unit 2, I explored forecasting using moving averages and regression models. Example: Monthly sales data (₱): 50k, 55k, 52k, 60k, 62k, 65k.

  • 3-Month Moving Average: Next forecasts → ₱59k, ₱62k, ₱63k
  • Regression Model: Sales = 48,500 + 2,700×Month (R² ≈ 0.92)

Interpretation: The regression model fits well (high R²), showing that sales are consistently increasing each month. This helps in planning production and budgeting.

Midterm Case Study

Case Study: Quick Sale Mart – Inventory & Sales Forecasting

For the midterm, I analyzed a small grocery store’s sales performance. Using actual sales data, I applied:

  • Excel Solver → to optimize inventory orders while minimizing costs.
  • Forecasting models → to predict next month’s demand for top products.
  • Decision analysis → to balance bulk purchasing discounts vs. storage limitations.

Output & Interpretation: The Solver recommended purchasing fewer perishable items and focusing on high-demand goods. Forecasts predicted steady demand growth (approx. +5% monthly). This model helps the store avoid overstocking and improve profitability.

Reflection: This case study showed me how quantitative methods connect theory to business decision-making. It highlighted the importance of data-driven strategies in small businesses like Quick Sale Mart.
