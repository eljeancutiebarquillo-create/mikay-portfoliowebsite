Unit 1 — Optimization (Excel Solver)

Problem (student-facing)

A manufacturer produces Tables and Chairs. Profit: Tables ₱500, Chairs ₱300. Resources per unit: Carpentry & Finishing. Capacities: Carpentry=120 hrs, Finishing=60 hrs.

Formulation
Let x1 = # Tables
Let x2 = # Chairs

Maximize Z = 500x1 + 300x2
Subject to:
  3x1 + 2x2 ≤ 120   (carpentry)
  2x1 + 1x2 ≤ 60    (finishing)
  x1, x2 ≥ 0

Solver result (interpretation): Optimal solution: x1 = 20 tables, x2 = 0 chairs → Max profit ₱10,000. Recommendation: prioritize tables under current capacities.

Unit 2 — Statistics & Forecasting

Dataset

Monthly sales (₱): 50,000; 55,000; 52,000; 60,000; 62,000; 65,000

Results
  • 3-month moving average: Forecasts → 59,000; 62,000; 63,000
  • Regression: Sales = 48,500 + 2,700 × Month (R² ≈ 0.92)

Interpretation: Both indicate an upward trend. Regression gives a stronger long-term fit; MA smooths short-term changes.

Midterm Project — Case Study (Quick Sale Mart)

Case summary

Objective: reduce stockouts and minimize total inventory cost. Methods: EOQ formula, Solver for order allocation, and short-term demand forecasting for top items.

Key outputs
  • EOQ calculation (example): EOQ ≈ 2,449 units (for given D, S, H)
  • Solver recommendation: focus orders on high-turnover items; reduced perishable orders.

Reflection: Applying EOQ and Solver together showed how forecasting + inventory policy reduce cost and spoilage for a small store.
