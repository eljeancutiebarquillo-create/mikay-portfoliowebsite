About This Course

This portfolio contains outputs from my Quantitative Methods course, highlighting optimization, forecasting, and a midterm case study project. It serves as evidence of my learning journey and reflections throughout the semester.

Unit 1 - Optimization

Problem: Maximize profit from producing tables (₱500) and chairs (₱300) given constraints in carpentry (120 hrs) and finishing (60 hrs).

Optimal Solution (Excel Solver): 20 tables, 0 chairs → Profit = ₱10,000

Unit 2 - Statistics & Forecasting

Example Forecast: Monthly sales data (₱): 50k, 55k, 52k, 60k, 62k, 65k.

  • 3-Month Moving Average → Forecast: 59k, 62k, 63k
  • Regression Model: Sales = 48,500 + 2,700×Month (R² ≈ 0.92)

Midterm Project - Case Study

Case Study: Inventory optimization for furniture production.

Objective was to determine the most profitable production plan using Solver. Results showed that focusing on table production maximized profit under resource constraints.

Result: 20 tables, 0 chairs → Profit = ₱10,000

Reflection: This project showed how quantitative methods guide real business decisions by balancing resources, constraints, and profit goals.
