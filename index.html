Unit 1 - Optimization (Excel Solver)

Problem: A bakery produces cakes and cookies. Flour and sugar are limited. Objective: Maximize profit subject to resource constraints.

Formulation:
Max Z = 40Cakes + 30Cookies
Subject to flour & sugar limitations.

Interpretation: Solver identified the best production mix. This demonstrates how linear programming helps businesses maximize profit with limited resources.

Unit 2 - Statistics & Forecasting

Dataset: Monthly sales data (₱): 50k, 55k, 52k, 60k, 62k, 65k.

  • 3-Month Moving Average: Forecast → ₱59k, ₱62k, ₱63k
  • Regression Model: Sales = 48,500 + 2,700×Month (R² ≈ 0.92)

Interpretation: The regression model fits well (R² = 0.92), showing steady growth. This helps in production planning and budgeting decisions.

Midterm Case Study

Case Study: Quick Sale Mart – Inventory Optimization

Objective: To balance inventory costs with demand forecasting for a grocery store.

  • Excel Solver optimized orders to reduce storage waste.
  • Forecasting predicted +5% monthly sales growth for top products.
  • Decision analysis guided bulk purchase vs. storage trade-offs.

Interpretation: Results showed prioritizing high-demand items increased revenue and reduced spoilage. This approach connects quantitative methods to real business strategy.
